Here's what they're trying to take away:

Freedom of information

In free countries:

Lots of media, blogs, social networks.

In countries where the Internet is not free:

Sites are blocked not by the dozens but by the hundreds of thousands. Social media, blogs, and even VPNs are blocked.

Freedom of expression

In free countries:

You can write whatever you want (within the bounds of decency), argue, and look for arguments.

In countries with no freedom of speech:

You can go to jail for speaking out against state policy but also against «values» — or rather, what the state promotes under the guise of values.

Freedom from ideology

In free countries:

The state does not impose its point of view; there are debates on various political and economic topics in society.

In countries with no freedom from propaganda:

Political lessons are taught in schools and universities, and television is so bad that it is better to avoid turning on the TV at all.

Tor

Tor is a browser for both smartphones and computers. It works on the proxy principle: you access the Internet through someone else's computer in another part of the world.

Unlike Ceno, Tor is not as resistant to blocking, so we recommend installing both browsers and using the best one for you.
